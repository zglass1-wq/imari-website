The Weekend

Two hundred and fifty years in the making.

The Salute to America 250 is the most ambitious Independence Day the country has ever staged — a million people on the National Mall, military flyovers, and a fireworks finale billed as the largest pyrotechnics display in the history of the world. Every moment of it sits within two miles of one private acre in central Georgetown. Three days that begin with a private chef dinner and live jazz on the grounds, move through a cocktail pre-party and formal ball, and close with fireworks watched from a private charter on the Potomac.

Thursday · July 2

Dinner and Jazz at Imari

The weekend opens at the estate. A private chef moves through the regions of America — small plates, considered pairings, the story of our country told course by course. When the table clears, cocktails are poured and the main salon comes alive with live jazz. The night ends on your own terms, at home.

60 people

Friday · July 3

Pre-Party, then the Ball

A specialty cocktail hour on the estate — a bespoke patriotic menu, passed bites, and the ease of a private start to a formal night. When the moment is right, cars take you to the Official Countdown 250 Ball at the Washington Hilton. Your table is waiting. Afterward, the car brings you back while the rest of the city finds its way home.

30 people

Saturday · July 4

Barbeque and Fireworks on the Water

An elevated barbecue on the estate lawn — long tables, cold drinks, lawn games in the afternoon sun. Unhurried. As evening falls, your group boards a private boat charter on the Potomac steps from the estate. The Salute to America 250 fireworks open above the monuments and reflect in the river below, with nothing between you and the sky.

200 people
