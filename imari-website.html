The Estate

Imari Georgetown

Georgetown  ·  Washington, D.C.

Entirely Yours

For the duration of your stay

A 12,000 square foot Federal-period residence in the heart of Georgetown, restored with original architectural details intact. Three floors, six bedroom suites, 15 bathrooms, and a separate three-bedroom guest house — all on a single private acre.

The estate gardens were designed by landscape architect Rose Greely in 1930 and later updated by Perry Wheeler — the designer of the White House Rose Garden. Grand estate walls, manicured boxwoods, terraced grounds, a bowling green, a large herb garden, and covered fireside seating. A setting that cannot be replicated.

A personal concierge is on-site throughout your stay. Share your preferences in advance and the house is ready for them. Anything else that comes up is handled the moment you ask.

Safety and Security

The property is designed and vetted with heads of state and CEOs in mind. Guests arrive and depart within the grounds of the residence, completely out of public view. Perimeter cameras, alarm systems, and tailored access control are standard. The house is safe, private, and secure.

Confidentiality and Discretion

Every staff member and vendor who enters the property is bound by a confidentiality agreement. No photography, video, or audio recording by our team, ever. We ask guests to exercise similar discretion. Our goal is to protect your privacy and the integrity of the estate for every guest who stays here.
