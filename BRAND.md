# Imari Brand Reference — Website

> **Repo:** Website (`imari-website`) version. The sibling version of this file lives at `BRAND.md` in the `imari-house-manual` repo and is adapted for in-property guest-facing collateral. Brand-level guidance (palette, typography, logo, core voice principles) is shared between the two; UI patterns, copy patterns, and shipping checklists differ per repo.

Source of truth for brand application in this codebase. Consult before any styling, copy, or asset change. When in doubt, default to restraint.

This file pairs with `CLAUDE.md` (project conventions) and `IMARI_VOICES.md` (voice authority).

---

## 1. What this site is

The website for **Imari** — a network of private estates across the world in the best cities for exclusive use of its guests. Currently there is one operating estate in Georgetown Washington DC, a 12,000 sq ft historic estate on an acre of gardens offered for exclusive use. New York City, Upper East Side, will be opening later this year. The repo serves the public marketing site at `imari.cc` plus a set of password-gated landing pages shared with travel advisors, corporate planners, and individual prospects.

It is **not**:
- The in-property house manual (that's `imari-house-manual`).
- A booking platform.
- A blog.

It **is**:
- The first impression for guests, advisors, planners, and prospects evaluating Imari for a stay or an event.
- A persuasive document, but persuasive by restraint — facts, photographs, and proportion do the work.
- Browsed before contact, not used in moments of need.

This shapes everything. Brevity matters. Specificity matters. Photography quality matters. The page should feel like correspondence from the estate, not collateral from a sales team.

---

## 2. Brand Purpose

Imari is an absolute-luxury private estate hospitality brand. Each property is offered for exclusive use — guests have the entire estate, with full-service infrastructure (concierge, butler, chef, housekeeping, transportation) operating in the background.

The brand communicates taste, access, and logistical prowess without announcing any of those things. It rewards recognition. It does not demand attention.

## 3. Brand Attributes

- **Historic** — Centuries of lived life. Modernized while retaining original character.
- **Exclusive** — The guest and their party are the only guests. No lobby, no staff unless summoned, no imposed schedule.
- **Intentional** — Every detail chosen for a reason.
- **Discreet** — Private gates, concealed arrivals, discreet staff.
- **Timeless** — Designed to feel neither new nor old.

## 4. Brand Values

- **Arrival is an art.** The moment of crossing the threshold should be felt, not processed.
- **Luxury is restraint.** Precision over abundance. Say less. Choose more carefully.
- **Privacy is respect.** Not a security feature — a value.
- **Good service disappears.** Guests should feel looked after, never managed.

## 5. Voice & Tone

> **Precedence:** For depth on voice — named voices (Host Voice, etc.), smell tests, the full verb diet — see `IMARI_VOICES.md`. Where the two files conflict, **`IMARI_VOICES.md` wins on voice**. This section is the quick-reference layer.

### How we sound
Confident without confirmation. Warm without performance. The way a person of genuine consequence speaks.

### Which voice
The body of every page is written in **Host Voice** (defined in `IMARI_VOICES.md`) — the chief of staff briefing the principal, competent and unshowy. Hero taglines and closing lines lean into **Reflective Voice** (italic Cormorant Garamond, declarative, standing alone). Persuasive positioning paragraphs use **Argument Voice** — same narrator as Host, more emphatic register, still in service of facts. See `IMARI_VOICES.md` for the full set.

### How we write — DO
- Short, complete sentences. Let them land.
- Confident and conversational. Assured, not stiff.
- Trust the reader. Do not tell them something is extraordinary.
- Restraint as a signal. Saying less implies more.
- Present tense. Imari exists now.
- Active voice. ("Your car arrives at 7:00" — not "It has been arranged.")

### How we write — DON'T
- **Never** use "bespoke," "curated," "world-class," or "unparalleled."
- **"luxury"** — capped at twice per piece, ideally zero. The phrase **"Comfortable luxury"** is the sanctioned brand-ethos line and is always allowed.
- **Avoid feeling-verbs** — *elevate, savor, experience, transform, indulge, enjoy* and the rest of the verb diet. See `IMARI_VOICES.md` for the full list.
- **Never** use exclamation marks.
- **Em-dashes** — allowed sparingly for rhythm (the brand uses them on imari.cc). Never as a substitute for a period or where a comma will do.
- **Never** use passive voice when active will do.
- **Never** explain the joke. If copy needs a footnote, rewrite the copy.
- **Never** apologize for things that haven't gone wrong.
- **Never** end with "Please don't hesitate to reach out."
- **Never** use marketing CTAs: "Book now," "Inquire today," "Limited availability."

**Functional CTAs are different.** *"Ask the concierge,"* *"Call extension 0,"* *"Message us"* are operational instructions, not sales asks. They are allowed, but lean declarative by default. Use the imperative only when it is genuinely clearer (urgent contacts, numbered instruction steps). Prefer *"The concierge handles dietary preferences"* over *"Ask the concierge about dietary preferences"* in prose; reserve imperatives for moments that need them.

### Website-specific guidance

This is persuasive content, not reference content. The voice rules above still apply, but pages have their own pattern:

- **Lead with the proposition, not the setup.** A planner lands on `/corporate.html` to see whether Imari fits their use case. The first screen tells them what the property is and what it offers — not "Welcome to Imari" boilerplate.
- **Section headings are nouns or short phrases.** "The Property," "The Service," "The Venue." Not commands, not questions.
- **Body paragraphs are short and declarative.** Three-sentence paragraphs at most for prose. One-line bullets in the offering particulars.
- **Numbers and specifics over adjectives.** "12,000 sq ft on one private acre" beats "expansive." "Six suites and three additional guest bedrooms" beats "ample accommodations."
- **No marketing CTAs.** No "Book now," "Inquire today," "Limited availability." The closing contact line at the bottom of each page is enough.
- **Personalized pages address the recipient by name in the hero eyebrow** ("prepared for ALEX ENDO") and otherwise read like any other Imari page. Don't overdo the personalization.

### Tone calibration — read imari.cc first

The marketing site at `imari.cc` is the highest-fidelity reference for the Imari voice in production. The house manual should sound like the same brand wrote both. Before writing or rewriting copy, read these excerpts and use them as a tuning fork.

**Specificity over adjectives:**

> "A 12,000 square foot Federal-period residence in the heart of Georgetown, restored with original architectural details intact. Three floors, six bedroom suites, 15 bathrooms, and a separate three-bedroom guest house — all on a single private acre."

Concrete numbers and proper nouns do the work. No "stunning," no "exquisite," no "breathtaking."

**Sentences that land:**

> "A private estate with a full staff. Entirely yours."

> "Twelve thousand square feet of main residence and a separate guest house. Six suites and three additional guest bedrooms. A chef's kitchen for the chefs you bring in, and a ballroom proportioned for celebrations at scale."

Short. Declarative. Trust the reader to feel the weight.

**Service described by what it does, not how it feels:**

> "A 24/7 concierge and lifestyle team manages logistics, last-minute requests, and the experiences you ask us to build. Butler service throughout the day. In-house event planning and production, up to the hour."

Notice what is missing: no "dedicated," no "passionate," no "exceptional." The work is described. The adjectives stay home.

**Em-dashes for rhythm (sparingly):**

> "The estate is yours for the duration. No shared spaces, no other guests."

> "An IV infusion, acupuncture, massage, contrast therapy, or simply an hour of meditation in the greenhouse. Recovery and care on your schedule, within the estate."

Em-dashes are used for pacing. Not as a substitute for a period or comma. Sentences breathe.

**Permission, not promotion:**

> "Yours to use however you like, whenever you like."

> "Ask for Anything."

The reader is invited, not sold to.

**Apply this to the house manual.** Where marketing copy describes the estate, the house manual describes its operation. Same voice, different subject. The Wi-Fi page is not the place for poetry — but the lead sentence on the Wi-Fi page should still sound like the same writer wrote imari.cc.

### Reference: lxiv voice vs. Imari voice

Real examples from the current lxiv site, rewritten in the Imari voice. Use them as a calibration tool alongside the imari.cc excerpts above.

| lxiv (current) | Imari (target) |
|----------------|----------------|
| "The property is totally kitted-out with the best in hi-fi and video equipment. We greatly encourage you to enjoy the setup and listen to as much music as possible!" | "The house is wired for music in every room. Sign into Wi-Fi, then cast to the room you are in." |
| "We have carefully curated everything in the house from the soaps to the wallpaper. If there is anything you are interested in, let us know and we will arrange to have it shipped to you." | "Most of what you see in the house is available to take home. Mention what you would like to the concierge." |
| "The entire property is secured by bespoke access control. We will help you get setup when you arrive and will add any guests that arrive later as well." | "The estate is secured by digital access control. Staff sets up your access on arrival and adds guests at your request." |
| "Our goal is to make sure that everything you want or need is attended to. To this end our staff is available any time to help you with your requests." | "Staff is available around the clock. You will see them throughout the day. Ask any of them for anything." |

## 6. Color Palette

All colors are from the Imari palette. Do not introduce colors outside this list.

| Name | Hex | Role | CSS variable |
|------|-----|------|--------------|
| Ink | `#000000` | Primary text on light backgrounds. Logo on light backgrounds. | `--ink` |
| Ember | `#1C1A15` | Dark surface color. Background behind Parchment logo. | `--ember` |
| Tobacco | `#6B5E4E` | Secondary text, dividers, muted UI. | `--tobacco` |
| Lichen | `#8A9A7A` | Accent — sparingly. Monogram leaf color. | `--lichen` |
| Terra cotta | `#855031` | Accent — sparingly. | `--terra-cotta` |
| Ochre | `#B8922E` | Accent. Used for thin decorative rules on imari.cc. | `--ochre` |
| Stone | `#C8BFB0` | Soft neutral backgrounds, dividers. | `--stone` |
| Parchment | `#F0EBE3` | **Primary page background.** | `--parchment` |
| Linen | `#F7F4EF` | Alternate light background, cards. | `--linen` |

The CSS variable names match `imari.cc` exactly. Keep them that way — it lets snippets move between the two sites without rewrites.

### Application rules
- Page background: **Parchment** `#F0EBE3`. (Note: `imari.cc` uses **Ember** for the marketing site — the manual is the inverted "daytime" surface of the same brand.)
- Body text: **Ink** `#000000` on Parchment.
- Muted text (metadata, timestamps, secondary nav): **Tobacco** `#6B5E4E`.
- Dark sections (hero, footer if used): **Ember** `#1C1A15` with **Parchment** or **Linen** text.
- Accents (Lichen, Terra cotta, Ochre): seasoning only. One accent per view, at most.
- **Ochre `#B8922E`** has a specific role on imari.cc: 1px decorative rules and the border on the "Inquire" button. Useful pattern to mirror for any restrained accent line in the manual.
- No pure white `#FFFFFF`. Use Linen.

## 7. Typography

### Typefaces
- **Cormorant Garamond** — headlines, page titles, section headers. Italics for emphasis.
- **Jost** — body copy on this repo. (Montserrat is the brand's web-wide alternate; Jost is what every page in this repo currently loads. Frutiger is the print alternate.)

### Google Fonts loader (matches imari.cc exactly)
```html
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.googleapis.com">
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.gstatic.com" crossorigin>
<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Cormorant+Garamond:ital,wght@0,300;0,400;0,500;1,300;1,400&family=Jost:wght@300;400;500&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">
```

The query string matches what every page in this repo loads. Keep it that way for sibling-site glyph consistency.

### Type scale (mobile-first; bump up at md/lg breakpoints)
- H1 / page title: Cormorant Garamond, 36–56px, weight 400, letter-spacing -0.01em.
- H2 / section: Cormorant Garamond, 26–34px, weight 400.
- H3 / subsection: Cormorant Garamond, 20–24px, often italic.
- Body: Jost, 16–17px, weight 300, line-height 1.6.
- Small / labels (e.g. "LAST UPDATED"): Jost, 11–12px, weight 500, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.14em, color Tobacco. On imari.cc some labels use letter-spacing as wide as 0.35em on small caps — adopt the same generous tracking for our small labels.

Use `clamp()` for fluid typography between mobile and desktop sizes, matching imari.cc's approach.

### Rules
- Headlines in Cormorant Garamond. Always.
- Body in Jost. Always.
- Line-height generous for body (1.55–1.7).
- Reading measure 60–75 characters per line for prose paragraphs.

## 8. Logo Usage

### Files (live in [logos/](logos/) at the repo root)
- `imari-ember.svg` — Ink/dark wordmark for light backgrounds.
- `imari-parchment.svg` — Light wordmark for dark backgrounds.
- `imari-gingko.svg` — Gingko leaf mark, used as favicon and decorative element (matches imari.cc).
- `imari-monogram.svg` — Available if needed.

SVG logos live in [logos/](logos/) at the repo root and are referenced from `https://imari-website-beta.vercel.app/logos/...` in every protected page (so the same image URL works from any sibling Vercel preview).

### Rules
- Always use the SVG logo files. Never type "Imari" in a font where the logo belongs.
- On Parchment/Linen backgrounds → use **Ember** (dark) logo.
- On Ember/Ink backgrounds → use **Parchment** (light) logo.
- Clear space around the logo: at least the height of the "I" on all sides.
- Header/nav logo: small. Around 80–120px wide on mobile, 140–180px on desktop. Not the focal point.
- Do not stretch, recolor, or add effects.
- The gingko mark works well as a favicon and as a subtle decorative element. Do not overuse.

## 9. UI Patterns for the Website

### Layout
- Full-bleed hero on every landing page. Imagery (or textured Ember background for event pages) + headline + short stat row.
- Content sections alternate between Parchment and Parchment-dark backgrounds to chunk the page.
- Max section width ~1200px for grids and galleries; ~880px for prose-heavy sections.
- Section padding 5–7rem on desktop, 5rem on mobile.
- Mobile-first. Most advisors and prospects read on phones first.

### Navigation
- Persistent fixed nav: logo (left), phone number + hamburger toggle (right).
- Hamburger opens a right-side drawer with section anchors for in-page jumps (`Galleries`, `The Property`, `The Service`, etc.) plus a separate "Plan a Stay" group.
- The home page at `imari.cc` uses a simpler nav variant — that's the marketing convention and is not what protected/personalized pages follow.

### Page chrome
- Hero: image or textured background, eyebrow line, Cormorant Garamond title with italic accent in Gold-light, Jost sub-paragraph, four-cell stat row.
- Closing: dark-image overlay with italic Cormorant tagline and contact link.
- Footer: Ember strip with small logo, copyright, and confidentiality note.

### Components
- **Galleries** — interior / exterior / site-plan grids with thumbnail rows + "View all N →" link that opens a full-screen lightbox.
- **Use-case sections** — image + body two-column grid, alternating sides, with section number, title, description, and bulleted list.
- **Offering particulars** — two-column key/value list with thin gold dividers.
- **Day cards** (event pages) — image + date + title + description + meta count.
- **Suite cards** — grid of two columns, parchment-dark background, ordered by tier.
- **Service tables** — three-column tables (service / price / unit), see [services-table.css](services-table.css).
- **Dividers** — 1px `var(--rule)` lines between rows and section grids.

### Motion
- Slow, restrained. 300–500ms ease-out for nav state changes, drawer slides, hover transitions.
- Thumbnail hover: `scale(1.04)` with 0.5s ease; subtle brightness changes on photo bands.

### Forbidden
- Gradients, glassmorphism, neon, drop shadows beyond the most subtle.
- Emoji in UI.
- "Read More" buttons. Show the content or link the section.
- Marketing CTAs in copy or button labels.
- Stock photography.

## 10. Imagery

The website uses property imagery extensively. Photography is the single strongest argument the site makes — invest in it.

- **Sensorial** — texture, materials, light. Detail over overview.
- **Inviting** — no ultra-wides without intent, no drone. Human-scale framing.
- **Detailed** — one object in the right light beats a full room.
- **Evocative** — the guest is implied, never shown. No people in frame.
- Warm, natural light. Never stock photography.

Photos serve from the Pixieset CDN (`images-pw.pixieset.com/elementfield/...`) for the main interior and exterior galleries; everything else lives in [photos/](photos/) and is served via `imari-website-beta.vercel.app/photos/...`. Site-plan diagrams live in `photos/`.

Hero images carry a gradient overlay so headline text stays readable. Photo bands and gallery thumbnails are slightly desaturated and brighten on hover.

## 11. Shipping checklist

Before any page goes live:

- [ ] Background is Parchment (or Ember for dark sections).
- [ ] Headlines are Cormorant Garamond. Body is Jost (this repo's default; Montserrat is the brand-wide alternate).
- [ ] No banned words: bespoke, curated, world-class, unparalleled.
- [ ] "Luxury" used zero or once at most (twice only if necessary). "Comfortable luxury" allowed.
- [ ] No feeling-verbs (elevate, savor, experience, transform, etc. — see IMARI_VOICES.md).
- [ ] No exclamation marks anywhere.
- [ ] Em-dashes used sparingly, for rhythm only.
- [ ] Active voice throughout.
- [ ] Hero leads with the proposition; no "Welcome to Imari" framing on inner pages.
- [ ] Functional CTAs lean declarative; imperatives reserved for moments that need them.
- [ ] Logo is from SVG file, not typed.
- [ ] No more than one accent color in view.
- [ ] CSS variable names match imari.cc (`--ink`, `--ember`, `--parchment`, etc.).
- [ ] Reads cleanly on a phone in portrait.
- [ ] Hero and gallery imagery loads sharp at retina sizes.
- [ ] Hamburger drawer anchors resolve to existing section IDs on the page.
